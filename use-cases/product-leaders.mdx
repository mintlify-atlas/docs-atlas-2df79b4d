## Overview

Whether helps product managers and product leaders build strategic habits by translating market climate signals into actionable roadmap guidance. Instead of operating on gut feel or internal capacity constraints alone, you can align feature priorities with real-time market conditions.

## Key workflows

### Start planning with climate context

Before roadmap grooming or quarterly planning, read the current market climate to understand:
- **Risk tolerance**: How aggressive should your product bets be right now?
- **Hiring pace**: Can you scale the team, or should you optimize with current capacity?
- **Roadmap aggressiveness**: Should you prioritize core reliability, expansion bets, or exploratory work?

Whether converts Treasury yield data and companion macro signals into explicit operating constraints, helping you avoid two common mistakes:
1. Operating like capital is easy when conditions are tightening
2. Over-correcting into defensive mode when conditions are supportive

### Shift from features to portfolio posture

Instead of treating all features equally, rebalance your roadmap mix based on climate guidance:

**In supportive climates:**
- Increase allocation to expansion bets and new market experiments
- Accelerate time-to-market for differentiation features
- Explore adjacent product opportunities

**In tightening climates:**
- Prioritize core reliability and customer retention features
- Focus on measurable payback and revenue protection
- Defer exploratory work until conditions improve

Explain trade-offs with market-aware reasoning: "We're prioritizing retention over expansion because credit spreads widened 40bps and funding velocity dropped 30% this quarter."

### Use Decision Shield for product bets

Before committing to major product bets, pressure-test them against downside scenarios:

1. **Lock your assumptions**: Document the market conditions and team capacity assumptions behind your bet
2. **Preview scenarios**: See how your roadmap holds up if conditions tighten unexpectedly
3. **Set reversal triggers**: Define what signals would cause you to pause or pivot

This builds decision quality, not just confidence. You can show executives that you've considered downside risks and have contingency plans ready.

### Create repeatable strategy narratives

Use Whether's brief exports to communicate:
- **Why priorities changed**: "We shifted focus from expansion to retention because HY credit spreads crossed our risk threshold"
- **What signals support the shift**: Citation-ready macro data with source timestamps
- **How we're tracking over time**: Historical context to demonstrate strategic maturity across climate regimes

## Real workflow example

From initial climate read to executive narrative:

```
1. Read current market climate
   → Regime: "Tightening"
   → Risk appetite: 35/100
   → Tightness: 72/100

2. Convert to operating posture
   → Hiring: Freeze new headcount
   → Roadmap: Prioritize retention over expansion
   → Risk tolerance: Defer platform rewrites

3. Rebalance portfolio
   → Move 3 expansion features to backlog
   → Accelerate 2 retention features
   → Add monitoring for usage patterns

4. Generate executive brief
   → "Q2 roadmap rebalanced toward retention
      based on 40bps widening in HY spreads
      and 30% drop in VC funding velocity.
      Deferred marketplace expansion until
      risk appetite recovers above 50."
```
